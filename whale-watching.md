      <h2>Overview</h2>
      <p>
        Every year from January to March, thousands of humpback whales arrive in Samaná Bay
        to mate, give birth, and rest in its warm, protected waters. Our tour takes you into
        this sanctuary with <strong>certified local guides</strong> and a strong commitment to
        responsible whale watching.
      </p>

      <p>
        We keep respectful distances, avoid sudden movements, and follow official regulations
        so the whales can be observed without stress — perfect for photographers, nature lovers,
        and families with children.
      </p>

      <h2>Itinerary (Typical)</h2>
      <ol class="itinerary">
        <li><strong>Check-in &amp; briefing</strong> at the pier in Santa Bárbara de Samaná.</li>
        <li><strong>Departure by boat</strong> into Samaná Bay with safety instructions.</li>
        <li><strong>Whale watching time</strong> with commentary from your guide about behavior, migration, and conservation.</li>
        <li><strong>Possible stop</strong> near Cayo Levantado (Bacardí Island) for photos from the boat (weather &amp; time permitting).</li>
        <li><strong>Return to Samaná pier</strong> and free time to explore the town or continue with another excursion.</li>
      </ol>

      <h2>What’s Included</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>Boat and certified captain.</li>
        <li>Official whale watching guide (English/Spanish).</li>
        <li>Life jackets for all passengers.</li>
        <li>Soft drinks and water on board.</li>
        <li>Whale watching regulations briefing.</li>
        <li>Insurance and marine park fees (if applicable).</li>
      </ul>
