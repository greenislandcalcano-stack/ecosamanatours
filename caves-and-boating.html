Overview

This experience takes you deep into the world-famous Los Haitises National Park, one of the Dominican Republic’s most extraordinary natural reserves. Navigate through lush mangrove forests, towering limestone formations, and hidden caves once used by the Taíno people.

The name “Haitises” means “mountainous land”, inspired by the park’s iconic green islands rising dramatically from the sea. This protected ecosystem is home to rich biodiversity, coastal lagoons, and some of the most photogenic landscapes in the Caribbean.

Itinerary (Typical)

  1. Departure by boat from Samaná pier.
  2. Navigation through mangroves and limestone formations (“mogotes”).
  3. Stop at caves to explore hidden chambers filled with Taíno pictographs, petroglyphs, and natural rock formations.
  4. Wildlife spotting: birds, marine life, and unique coastal species.
  5. Optional swim or beach stop depending on conditions.
  6. Return cruise to the pier and typical local lunch.
  7. Transfer back to your hotel.
