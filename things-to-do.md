      <h2>🌿 The Story of Samaná</h2>
      <p class="history-lead">
        Welcome to Samaná — a peninsula of hidden waterfalls, colorful villages, old ports,
        seafaring stories, and unforgettable landscapes. At EcoSamana Adventures, we love sharing
        not only the beauty of this paradise, but also the story of the people and places that make it so special.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="history-parchment reveal-up">
      <div class="parchment-badge">📜 A Living Story</div>
      <h3>From Bay Legends to Railway Glory</h3>
      <p>
        Samaná is more than a beautiful destination — it is a place where history, culture, trade,
        and nature have met for centuries. Here, the sea was a highway, the forests were a refuge,
        and the villages each grew with their own character and charm.
      </p>
      <p>
        Some places, like Santa Bárbara de Samaná, trace their roots deep into the colonial era.
        Others, like Sánchez, flourished later during the great age of railways and international trade.
        Together, they tell the story of a peninsula shaped by adventure, resilience, and connection to the world.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="history-grid">
      <article class="history-card reveal-up">
        <div class="history-icon">🌊</div>
        <h3>Santa Bárbara de Samaná</h3>
        <p>
          The heart of the peninsula and the soul of the bay. Santa Bárbara de Samaná grew as a strategic
          coastal settlement, surrounded by one of the most stunning bays in the Caribbean.
        </p>
        <p>
          Over time, it became a place of fishermen, merchants, travelers, and dreamers — and today it remains
          the vibrant center of life, culture, and adventure in the region.
        </p>
      </article>

      <article class="history-card reveal-up">
        <div class="history-icon">🚂</div>
        <h3>Sánchez</h3>
        <p>
          It is important to know that <strong>Sánchez did not exist during the colonial period</strong>.
          The town rose much later, in the late 19th century, during its celebrated
          <strong>Golden Age</strong>, driven by the railway and international commerce.
        </p>
        <p>
          As the principal export port for cacao and coffee from the Cibao, Sánchez became a lively cosmopolitan town.
          Ships arrived constantly, trade flourished, and the streets echoed with many languages.
        </p>
        <p>
          During its peak, foreign consulates and vice-consulates from
          <strong>Great Britain, Germany, France, the United States, Spain, the Netherlands, and Norway</strong>
          helped manage maritime trade and protect commercial interests.
        </p>
        <p>
          This global atmosphere left its mark on Sánchez, especially in its elegant
          <strong>Victorian-style wooden architecture</strong>, once home to diplomats, merchants, and international company managers.
        </p>
      </article>

      <article class="history-card reveal-up">
        <div class="history-icon">💦</div>
        <h3>El Limón</h3>
        <p>
          El Limón is where the peninsula turns green, wild, and magical. Long known as a rural farming community,
          it became famous for its lush tropical landscape and its spectacular waterfall hidden in the hills.
        </p>
        <p>
          Today, El Limón is one of the most beloved natural treasures of Samaná — a place where visitors follow trails,
          cross streams, and discover the refreshing power of nature.
        </p>
      </article>

      <article class="history-card reveal-up">
        <div class="history-icon">🌴</div>
        <h3>Las Terrenas</h3>
        <p>
          Once a quiet fishing village, Las Terrenas grew into one of the most colorful and international communities
          in the Dominican Republic.
        </p>
        <p>
          Caribbean warmth meets global charm here. Beaches, food, music, and cultures blend together, creating a town
          that feels both local and worldly at the same time.
        </p>
      </article>

      <article class="history-card reveal-up">
        <div class="history-icon">🐚</div>
        <h3>Las Galeras</h3>
        <p>
          At the edge of the peninsula lies Las Galeras, peaceful and untamed, where the land leans into the Atlantic
          and the scenery feels wonderfully wild.
        </p>
        <p>
          Once a quiet fishing settlement, it remains a gateway to hidden beaches, dramatic coastlines,
          and the more untouched side of Samaná.
        </p>
      </article>

      <article class="history-card reveal-up fun-fact-card">
        <div class="history-icon">✨</div>
        <h3>Fun History Facts</h3>
        <ul class="history-facts">
          <li>🚢 Samaná Bay has long been one of the most prized natural harbors in the Caribbean.</li>
          <li>🏠 Sánchez became famous for its cosmopolitan energy and elegant Victorian houses.</li>
          <li>🌿 El Limón blends farming traditions, forest trails, and waterfall adventure.</li>
          <li>🌍 Las Terrenas became a meeting point of Dominican and international cultures.</li>
          <li>🏝️ Las Galeras still feels like a secret corner of paradise.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>
    </div>

    <div class="history-parchment closing-parchment reveal-up">

        you are also traveling through the living story of Samaná.
      </p>
